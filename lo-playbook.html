The 3/1 and 5/1 Hybrid ARMs are one of your most powerful tools — especially when a fixed-rate refi doesn't pencil because the rate jump is too large. Master this product and you'll convert deals others walk away from.

📐 What is a Hybrid ARM — explain it simply
A Hybrid ARM has two phases: a fixed period where the rate doesn't move, and an adjustable period where it can change annually. The number before the slash is the fixed years. The number after is how often it adjusts.
Product Fixed Period Then Adjusts Best Use Case
3/1 ARM Fixed for 3 years Annually after year 3 Borrower plans to sell or refi within 3 yrs, or needs maximum payment savings now
5/1 ARM Fixed for 5 years Annually after year 5 More stability, still lower than fixed. Sweet spot for most borrowers. Easiest sell.
Key point for LOs: Most borrowers refinance again within 5–7 years anyway. The 5/1 ARM often covers their entire realistic holding period — and they get a lower rate the whole time.
🔒 How caps protect the borrower — know this cold
Every ARM has a caps structure that limits how much the rate can move. The most common is 2/2/5 or 5/2/5. You need to understand and be able to explain this without hesitation.
CapWhat It LimitsExample (2/2/5 on a 6% start rate)
Initial Cap Max increase at first adjustment Can only go to 8% at first adjustment
Periodic Cap Max increase each year after that Can only go up 2% per year from there
Lifetime Cap Max rate over the life of the loan Can never exceed 11% total (6 + 5)
Say it like this:
"Even in a worst-case scenario — rates spike and your loan adjusts as high as it possibly can — the most your rate could ever reach is [X]%. That's not a guess, it's written into your loan contract. And you'd have to be in this loan for 5 years past the fixed period for that to even happen."
⏱️ When to introduce the ARM on a call
Don't lead with the ARM — introduce it as a solution when the fixed rate refi creates an objection. These are your trigger moments:
TRIGGER: Payment objection
Borrower says: "My payment would go up too much" or "The rate isn't low enough to make this worth it"
TRIGGER: Rate comparison objection
Borrower says: "I already have a good rate" or "I'm not going to go higher"
YOUR PIVOT:
"I hear you — and a fixed rate refi may not be the right move. But here's something that might change the picture: we also have a hybrid ARM option that gives you a fixed rate for 5 years at a lower rate than a 30-year fixed. It's not for everyone, but if you're planning to be in the home for 5 years or less, or if you want the lower payment to consolidate debt, it can actually work really well. Can I show you what those numbers look like alongside the fixed?"

💬 How to present ARM vs Fixed side-by-side
Walk the borrower through this comparison verbally. Fill in the real numbers from your scenario tool.
"Let me show you two options side by side. On a 30-year fixed, your rate would be [X]% and your payment would be [Y]. On the 5/1 ARM, your rate for the first 5 years is [X-lower]% and your payment is [Y-lower]. That's [difference] a month in savings. Over 5 years, you'd save [5yr savings] in payments. Even if you refinanced at year 5, you'd still come out ahead compared to paying the higher fixed rate the whole time. Now if rates go way up and you need to refinance at year 5, that's a risk — but most people in your situation are going to refinance again before year 5 anyway. And the worst-case scenario on this ARM is a rate cap of [lifetime max]%. Does the lower payment change the picture for you?"
