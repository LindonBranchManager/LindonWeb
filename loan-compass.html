📖 Key Terms Glossary — click to expand ▼
DTI (Debt-to-Income)
All monthly debts + new housing payment ÷ gross income. VA guideline: ≤41% (higher OK with residual income). FHA: up to ~57% with AUS. Conventional: up to ~50% with AUS.
LTV (Loan-to-Value)
Loan amount ÷ appraised value. 95% LTV = 5% down. Lower LTV = better pricing, lower PMI, stronger loan.
FHA MIP
FHA mortgage insurance. Two parts: UFMIP (1.75% upfront, financed in) + annual MIP (~0.55%/yr, paid monthly). If LTV was over 90% at closing, MIP lasts the ENTIRE life of the loan.
PMI (Conventional)
Private Mortgage Insurance on conventional loans when LTV is above 80%. Unlike FHA MIP, PMI automatically cancels when LTV reaches 80% — a huge long-term advantage.
VA Funding Fee
One-time fee financed into a VA loan. Ranges 1.4%–3.6% based on down payment and first vs. subsequent use. Veterans with 10%+ disability rating pay $0.
LLPA
Loan Level Price Adjustments — extra fees on conventional loans charged by Fannie/Freddie based on credit score and LTV. Lower credit + higher LTV = higher LLPA = effectively a higher rate. This is why FHA sometimes wins at lower credit scores.
IRRRL (VA Streamline)
VA to VA only. No appraisal (fixed rate). 0.5% funding fee. 1×30 late allowed past 12 months. Payment reduction rules: Fixed→Fixed = rate must drop. Fixed→ARM = 2% rate reduction required. ARM→Fixed = rate must drop. ARM→ARM = rate must drop.
AUS
Automated Underwriting System. VA uses LGY, FHA uses TOTAL Scorecard, Conventional uses DU (Fannie) or LP (Freddie). AUS can approve loans that exceed standard DTI guidelines with compensating factors.
Conforming Limit (2025)
$806,500 max for conventional in most counties. Above this = jumbo = stricter underwriting. VA has no loan limit with full entitlement. FHA limits vary by county.
ARM (Adjustable Rate Mortgage)
5/1 ARM = rate fixed for 5 years, then adjusts once per year. 3/1 ARM = fixed for 3 years, then annual adjustments. Adjustment is based on an index (like SOFR) plus a margin. Caps limit how much the rate can change per adjustment and over the life of the loan. Lower initial rate = lower initial payment — but risk increases after the fixed period.
Residual Income (VA)
After ALL payments, the veteran must have a set amount left over based on family size and region. This can save a VA loan that exceeds 41% DTI — always check it.

Sales Playbook & Presentation Guide

Scripts, comparisons, objection handlers, and income doc quick reference — click to expand

▼ Open
🎖️
The "You Earned It" VA Close
Always open a VA conversation by anchoring the benefit to the veteran's service. Many vets assume VA loans are complicated or have a catch.

"Mr./Ms. Borrower, this is a benefit you earned through your service. It costs nothing extra each month in mortgage insurance, requires no down payment, and often comes with the most competitive rate available. Let's make sure you're using everything you're entitled to."

Follow with the math: Pull up the MI comparison. Show them the monthly difference — no MIP, no PMI, $0/month. Let the numbers close for you.
🚫
The "MI Math" Story — Making MIP Real
Borrowers glaze over at percentages. Make it concrete with dollars and time.

"At $X/month in mortgage insurance, that's $X,XXX per year. Over 5 years that's $XX,XXX. With a VA loan, that number is exactly zero — every single month."

For FHA LTV > 90%: "I want to make sure you understand — with less than 10% down on an FHA loan, the mortgage insurance stays for the entire life of the loan. It never goes away unless you refinance. That's something we need to plan around."

For conventional PMI: "The difference with conventional PMI is that it cancels automatically when your equity hits 20%. You don't have to refinance or ask — it just stops."
🏠
The "FHA Bridge" Conversation
FHA is often the right short-term choice for credit-challenged borrowers. Present it as a strategy, not a consolation prize.

"Here's the plan: we use FHA to get you into the home today because conventional doesn't work yet. Then we build a 12–18 month roadmap — keep your credit clean, let the home appreciate, and when we hit 680+ credit and 20% equity, I'm going to call you and we'll refinance you into conventional and cut that MIP."

Why this works: It turns a transaction into a relationship. The borrower trusts that you're thinking about their long-term financial health, not just closing a loan.
📊
The "5-Year True Cost" Close
Borrowers obsess over rate. Train them to think in total cost.

"Instead of looking at rates, let's look at what you'll actually spend over the next 5 years. That includes the monthly payment, all mortgage insurance, and the upfront costs. Here's what each option actually costs you."

Use the 5-Year Total row in the results above. An option with a slightly higher rate but no MI will often beat a lower rate with MIP over 5 years.

Key insight: A 0.25% rate difference on a $400K loan = ~$60/month. But $150/month in MIP vs. $0 is a $9,000 difference over 5 years — 3× bigger impact.
🔄
Plant the IRRRL / Refi Seed at Closing
Never lose a client to another LO when rates drop. Set expectations at origination.

"Today we're getting you into a great loan. Here's my promise: when rates drop by half a point or more, I'm going to personally reach out to you. For VA borrowers there's a streamlined refinance called the IRRRL — minimal paperwork, no appraisal required, very fast. I want to be the person who saves you money down the road."

For FHA borrowers: "When we get you to 20% equity and the credit score where we want it, I'll call you about a conventional refi to drop that MIP. I'll be tracking that for you."
💳
The "Credit Score ROI" Pitch
Sometimes the best advice is to wait 60–90 days and improve the score. Frame this as financial coaching.

"Here's the honest truth — if we close today, you're looking at $X/month. If you spend 60 days paying down that credit card to under 30% utilization, your score should move 20–30 points. At 680+ credit, your PMI rate drops and you're saving $X/month for the life of the loan. That's potentially $XX,XXX over 5 years for 60 days of patience. What do you want to do?"

Score tier thresholds to know:
580 → FHA eligible (3.5% down)
620 → Conventional opens up
680 → PMI rates meaningfully better
740 → Best conventional LLPA tier

🎙️ Script — Walking a Borrower Through the Table
"Let me show you three options side by side. The VA loan is on the left — it's only available to you because of your service, and as you can see, the mortgage insurance column is zero. That's the biggest difference. FHA in the middle works well if credit is a factor, but the mortgage insurance can last the life of the loan. Conventional on the right has PMI but it cancels when you hit 20% equity. Let me show you what each one actually costs you per month with your specific numbers..."
Tip: Always run the numbers first (in Loan Compass), then walk through this comparison. The math does the selling — you're just the guide.

Click any objection to see the recommended response. These are designed to be conversational, not scripted — adapt them to your natural voice.

❓"Your rate is higher than what I saw online / another lender quoted me."
"I completely understand — rates online are always the headline number. What they don't show is the full picture. Let me show you the total monthly payment including mortgage insurance and all costs. A rate that's 0.125% lower but comes with $200/month in extra MI fees actually costs you more. Let's compare apples to apples." Pull up the total monthly breakdown in Loan Compass. Show the 5-year total cost. Often the "lower rate" loan costs significantly more when MI is factored in.
❓"I'm going to wait until rates drop before I buy."
"That makes sense emotionally, but let's run the math together. If home prices in this market appreciate even 4% this year, a $400,000 home becomes $416,000. That $16,000 in price increase equals about $80/month in extra principal — and you'd need rates to drop roughly 0.5% just to offset that. Plus you've been renting this whole time." "Here's the other piece: when rates do drop, we can refinance you — especially on a VA loan with the IRRRL. You're not locked in forever. The question isn't 'should I wait for the perfect rate' — it's 'should I start building equity today or keep paying someone else's mortgage?'"
❓"I don't want to pay PMI / mortgage insurance."
"You're right that it's a cost — let me show you exactly what it costs and when it goes away. With conventional, PMI cancels automatically when you hit 20% equity. With a $400K home appreciating at even 3% per year plus your regular payments, you could be there in 4–6 years. The question is whether putting more money down today to avoid PMI is a better use of that cash than keeping it as reserves or investments." "For VA-eligible borrowers: there's actually a way to have zero mortgage insurance — the VA loan. Your service already paid for that benefit."
❓"I've heard VA loans are harder to get accepted / sellers don't like them."
"That was more true 10–15 years ago. Today's market is very different — sellers see VA offers all the time and most listing agents are VA-experienced. The VA appraisal is thorough but protects the veteran from overpaying. And from an underwriting standpoint, VA loans have very strong guidelines." "Here's the reality: a pre-approved VA buyer is a strong, qualified buyer. In competitive markets, what matters is price and terms — not the loan type. I'll help you write a competitive offer and communicate clearly with the listing agent."
❓"I only want a 30-year fixed. I don't like the idea of an ARM."
"That's a completely reasonable position — the 30-year fixed is the most popular mortgage in America for good reason. Let me just show you one comparison. On a 5/1 ARM, the rate is locked for 5 full years. If you're planning to sell or refinance before that fixed period ends, you get the benefit of the lower rate with none of the adjustment risk." "The key question is your time horizon: how long do you plan to be in this specific home? If it's 10+ years, I'd lean toward fixed with you. If it's 3–7 years, the ARM is worth a conversation."
❓"My credit isn't great. Can I even qualify?"
"Let's look at what you actually qualify for — you might be surprised. FHA opens at 580 with 3.5% down, and VA has no hard minimum if you're a veteran. Even a 620 score opens conventional options." "If you're below those thresholds, let me give you a 60-day action plan. Paying down credit card balances below 30% of the limit can move a score 20–40 points — sometimes in one billing cycle. Let's run your scenario today and I'll show you what changes between now and hitting that next credit tier."
❓"What's the catch with the VA loan?"
"Honest answer: not much. The main things to know are: (1) there's a one-time funding fee — typically 2.6% for first-time use — that's financed into the loan, not paid at closing. Veterans with a disability rating of 10%+ are exempt from it entirely. (2) The property has to meet VA's Minimum Property Requirements, which are designed to protect the veteran from buying a home with major defects." "In exchange for those two things, you get: no down payment required, no monthly mortgage insurance ever, and often the most competitive rate on the market. Most veterans who use their VA benefit say they wish they'd used it sooner."
🗝️
Qualify the Borrower's Time Horizon First
Before presenting an ARM, always establish how long they plan to stay.

"Before I show you this option, I need to ask — what's your plan for this home? Are you planning to be here long-term or is this more of a 5–7 year situation?"

If < 5 years: 3/1 or 5/1 ARM is legitimate
If 5–8 years: 5/1 ARM worth discussing
If 8+ years: Lean toward fixed — explain rate risk

Never present an ARM to a borrower who plans to stay long-term without clearly explaining the rate adjustment risk and caps.
📉
The ARM Value Proposition Script
"A 5/1 ARM means your rate is completely fixed — locked in — for the first 5 years. Not adjusting, not changing. Fixed. After 5 years it can adjust annually based on the market index plus our margin."

"The lower initial rate means a lower payment right now. For someone planning to sell or refinance in under 5 years, you get all the savings with none of the adjustment risk."

Always explain rate caps:
Typical VA/FHA/Conv ARM caps: 1/1/5
Year 6 max increase: +1%
Each year after: +1%
Lifetime cap: +5% over start rate
🔒
ARM Risk Disclosure — Say It Plainly
Always disclose the risk clearly and document that you did. New LOs sometimes underemphasize this — don't.

"I want to make sure you understand: after the fixed period ends, your rate will adjust up or down based on the market. In a rising rate environment, your payment could increase. In a falling rate environment, it could decrease."

"Here's the worst-case scenario with this loan — if rates went up as much as the caps allow, your payment would become approximately $X/month. Are you comfortable with that number?"

If they're uncomfortable with the worst case: stop and sell fixed.
⚠️
When NOT to Recommend an ARM
Some situations where fixed is clearly the right call:

❌ Fixed income borrowers (retirees, disability income) — payment predictability is critical
❌ Tight DTI qualification — already borderline; rate increase could default them
❌ Confirmed long-term plans — "this is our forever home"
❌ First-time buyers with anxiety — stability matters for comfort
❌ Volatile rate environment — if rates trending up, fixed locks in today's rate

When in doubt, fixed is the conservative, defensible recommendation.

Texas Loan Rules
Cash Out on Government Loans: Not allowed in Texas. Cash out must be done via Conventional only.

Discount Points on TX Cash Out: Cannot be financed. If doing a TX Cash Out for reasons that disqualify the IRRRL, the borrower must bring discount points to closing in cash.

Flag Texas loans immediately at intake — these rules affect the loan structure significantly.
💍
Non-Purchasing Spouse States
In these community property states, a non-purchasing spouse must sign specific documents even if they're not on the loan:

Arizona, California, Idaho, Louisiana, Nevada, New Mexico, Texas, Washington, Wisconsin

Always confirm marital status and state of property at intake. Forgetting this causes underwriting delays at the worst possible time.
🔄
Streamline Rules
VA IRRRL Net Tangible Benefit Rules:
Fixed → Fixed: Rate must decrease (must be current)
Fixed → ARM: 2% interest rate reduction required
ARM → Fixed: No reduction required
ARM → ARM: rate must decrease
Funding fee: 0.5% flat. 1×30 late allowed past 12 mo.
Eligible occupancy: Primary, 2nd Home, Investment.

FHA Streamline: Must be FHA to FHA. No lates in past 12 months. No cash out allowed. No appraisal. Cannot finance discount points.

Conv or Gov-to-Conv: Always a full document loan. No streamline option.
